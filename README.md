# 🔧 Analog Function Generator | Semester 3 Project

As part of our Semester 3 coursework, we successfully designed and built an Analog Function Generator using only analog components, meeting all specified performance requirements.

## 🔹 Project Objectives

- Generate sine, square, triangular, and sawtooth waveforms
- Adjustable frequency range: 20 Hz – 20 kHz
- Amplitude control for all waveforms
- Duty cycle control for square waves
- DC offset adjustment applicable to any waveform

## 🔹 Implementation & Design

Designed and fabricated three PCBs:

- **Main Function Generator PCB** – complete analog waveform generation and control (designed by @Heshan Ranasinghe)
- **AC–DC Power Supply PCB** – converting 230 V AC to 12 V DC (designed by @Dulen Sandiw)
- **12 V to 3.3 V Regulator PCB** – intended to power an ESP32 module for display and monitoring (designed by @Ahesh dilhan)

Although the ESP32-based display was planned, time constraints limited the implementation. Therefore, this project primarily showcases the PCB design and analog hardware development.

A custom enclosure was designed and fabricated, giving the final product a clean and professional finish (designed by @Nipun Rathnayake).

## 🔹 Team Contributions

- Waveform generation theory, schematic design, and circuit simulations were collaboratively handled by the entire team
- PCB design, power conversion, and enclosure design were distributed among team members based on expertise

## 🔹 Outcome

The final system operated successfully and reliably, meeting all functional requirements. This project strengthened our understanding of analog circuit design, PCB development, power electronics, and collaborative engineering workflows.
